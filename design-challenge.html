Design Brief: Memory Garden

Design a small memory garden concept that could help preserve a specific cultural tradition, skill, or story while creating meaningful connections between generations.

Your garden should intentionally use sensory elements (smell, touch, sight, sound) to trigger memories and facilitate the sharing of cultural knowledge.

Design Process

  1. 1

    Select a Tradition or Story

    Think about a skill, story, or tradition that an elder in your family or community knows, but that might be forgotten if not intentionally preserved.

  2. 2

    Identify Sensory Triggers

    What sensory elements are associated with this tradition? How might they trigger memories and stories?

  3. 3

    Design Your Memory Garden

    Create a concept design for your memory garden. You can use the blank space below to sketch your design or describe how the elements would be arranged.

    Garden Elements
    Sketch Your Design

    Use this space to sketch your design

    You can use any drawing tool or simply describe your design in the space below

  4. 4

    Explain How Your Design Facilitates Intergenerational Connection

    How will your memory garden create opportunities for elders to share knowledge with younger generations? How will it preserve the specific tradition you've chosen?
